I didn't have a plan when I started.

I had a dataset, a question I couldn't answer yet, and enough stubbornness to figure it out.

That was the beginning. One notebook. Then another. Then competitions. Then models that actually worked. Then models that *really* worked. Then hackathons where we shipped in 48 hours and slept later. Then 39 notebooks. Then 6,892 people said those notebooks helped them.

That's the whole story.

I'm **Saman Fatima** — data scientist, Kaggle Grandmaster, builder from Pakistan 🇵🇰

What I work on: exploratory analysis, classical ML, deep learning for vision and language, time-series, and lately a lot of LLM and agent work. I write notebooks the way I wished someone had written them for me when I was starting — reasoning first, results second, nothing hidden.

I also spend time helping people who are where I was. Because someone's confusion is always worth taking seriously.

### the hackathons

Four products. Built with small teams. Shipped under pressure. Here's what we made:

<br/>

**[Urdu Legal E-Commerce Agent](https://lablab.ai/ai-hackathons/raise-your-hack/binary-ninjas/legal-e-commerce-ai-agent)**
Pakistani legal documents are dense, technical, and written for lawyers. We built a multi-agent system that reads them and explains them in plain Urdu. Stack: Llama + Groq.

<br/>

**[Python Buddy](https://lablab.ai/ai-hackathons/co-creating-with-gpt-5/code-commanders/ai-master)**
An AI tutor that doesn't just give answers — it asks you questions back. Socratic hints, automated code coaching, and the patience of someone who actually wants you to understand.

<br/>

**[Predictive Network Maintenance](https://lablab.ai/ai-hackathons/aistronauts-space-agents-on-a-mission/ai-astronauts/predictive-network-maintenance-system)** · **[II](https://lablab.ai/ai-hackathons/ai-for-connectivity-hackathon/dreamscape-agenz/predictive-network-maintenance-system)**
Off-grid schools in underserved areas lose internet connectivity and nobody knows why until it's too late. We built systems to predict and prevent that — twice, across two hackathons, because the problem was worth solving again.
